What is Warm Intro Finder?

Warm Intro Finder helps you discover the shortest, strongest route through your network to reach anyone — by chaining together mutual connections on Twitter, Farcaster, LinkedIn, and on-chain wallets. Instead of guessing who knows whom, it shows you the path, the strength of each hop, and drafts the outreach message for you.

Why use it? Cold messages get ignored. A warm intro — "hey, we both know X" — gets a reply 3-5× more often. This tool turns every social graph you already have into a pathfinding problem and hands you the answer.

1

👤 Set up your profile

On the home page, click Edit profile and fill in your name plus any handles you use: Twitter, Farcaster, LinkedIn, or DeBank wallet. You don't need all four — add whichever you're on.

  • Only platforms you enter are used. If you only add a Twitter handle, the whole system focuses on Twitter — no Farcaster/LinkedIn/DeBank rows appear anywhere.
  • Different handles = different networks. The network is seeded from your exact handle mixHandle-specific seedEach handle+platform combination produces a deterministic network via SHA-256. Saving the same profile always reproduces the same people; swapping a handle gives you a brand-new network.. Switching handle creates a totally different 56-person network.
  • Clearing your profile clears all network data. Save with every handle blank and the CSVs are wiped — no stale connections linger.
Example: Save with Twitter @jenlim only → network only shows Twitter interactions. Add LinkedIn jen-lim and save again → both Twitter and LinkedIn activity appear.
2

📊 Understanding your network

Right under the profile card you'll see six stat tilesStats dashboardA live snapshot of your network — recomputed whenever you save your profile.:

  • People — total unique people discovered across all degrees.
  • Connections — total relationships (edges) between those people.
  • Strong — connections with strength score 10 or above.
  • Moderate — connections with strength 5–9.
  • Platforms — number of platforms represented in the network.
  • Avg Strength — the average strength across every connection.

Below the stats is the network graphNetwork graphVis-network visualization of your full 3-degree graph. Drag nodes, scroll to zoom, click Hide to collapse. — an interactive view you can drag, zoom, and hide. Edge colors match the strength bucket:

🟢 Green — Very Strong 🔵 Blue — Strong 🟡 Yellow — Moderate ⚪ Grey — Weak

A diamond shape marks you; every other person in your network is a circle sized by how many connections they have.

3

🔍 Finding a target person

Type into the Find target box. The finder matches against name, handle (with or without @), or wallet address — partial matches work.

The search covers every degree of your network (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Autocomplete suggestions include a chip showing that person's degree from you.

Example: type sofia, @pascal, or 0xabc — pick the autocomplete match, then press Find warm path.
4

🏆 Understanding the 3 ranked paths

The finder returns up to three routes, ranked by how easily you can reach the target:

  • 🥇 Best Path — recommended; fewest hops and strongest mutuals.
  • 🥈 Good Path — second-best alternative.
  • 🥉 Possible Path — a fallback if the first two don't work.

Hops means the number of people in the chain between you and the target — fewer hops = easier intro. Each hop has a colored arrow whose color matches the strength of that specific connection.

Click Select this path on any card to expand its outreach section. Or click 🔀 Compare Paths at the bottom to open a side-by-side view with these metrics:

👣 Steps to reach targetNumber of hops — fewer is easier.
💪 Connection StrengthVisual bar (Weak / Moderate / Strong / Very Strong) summing all hop strengths.
🎯 Success LikelihoodHigh (green) / Medium (yellow) / Low (red), based on the weakest hop.
📬 Best PlatformThe one platform most likely to deliver this intro.
💬 Interaction Quality🔥 High / 💪 Moderate / 👋 Low activity averaged across people in the path.
⭐ Why this pathA 1-2 sentence plain-English explanation per path.

Use ✓ Select Best Path inside the modal to jump straight to the recommended one.

5

📈 Understanding interaction scores

Each person has an interaction score (0–15)Interaction score0-15 scale — sums per-platform counts and caps at 15. Higher means more direct engagement between you and that person. aggregated from concrete signals on every platform they're on:

  • 👍 Likes — Twitter likes.
  • 💬 Comments / Replies — Twitter comments, Farcaster replies.
  • 🔁 Reposts / 🔄 Recasts — Twitter reposts and Farcaster recasts.
  • ⭐ Recommendations and 🏆 Endorsements — LinkedIn.
  • 💸 Transactions — on-chain interactions tracked via DeBank.

Each connection (edge) between two people is bucketed by strength:

🔥 Very Strong 12–15 💪 Strong 8–11 👋 Moderate 4–7 🤝 Weak 1–3

Under every path you'll see a mutual-platform panel: a green ✔ means both people are on that platform, a grey ✖ means one of them isn't. Only the platforms in your profile appear.

7

🎯 Tracking your outreach

Two indicators update automatically at the top of every results page:

  • Profile Visited — auto-ticks when you click any Open Platform link.
  • Message Sent — auto-ticks when you click Mark as Sent or submit the in-system composer.

Each outreach card also has a Warm Intro Progress bar that advances through four stages:

  • Stage 1: Message Sent — fires when you click Send Warm Intro Message or submit the composer.
  • Stage 2: Awaiting Reply — fires when you click Mark as Sent.
  • Stage 3: Meeting Scheduled — click this stage yourself once the reply turns into a meeting.
  • Stage 4: Goal Achieved — click when the intro delivers the outcome you wanted.

Your Total Outreach, Reply Rate, Meeting Rate, and Success Rate tiles (right under the stats dashboard on the home page) roll up live. These stats are scoped per handlePer-handle statsSwitching your profile to a different handle loads that handle's own history. Stats never mix between handles. — switching profile handle shows that handle's own history.

8

⏰ Best Time to Reach Out

Every outreach card starts with a Best Time banner — a quick recommendation for when to actually send the message.

  • Headline window — e.g. "Best time: Weekdays 9am–11am".
  • Reason sentence — e.g. "Based on 22 interactions, @kira is most active on Twitter during morning hours on weekdays."
  • Per-platform mini-cards — one card per platform the target is on, each with its own recommended window and short reason.

The suggestion is derived from:

  • the target's most-active platform (which one has the highest interaction count),
  • whether their last recorded interaction fell on a weekday or weekend,
  • and typical activity windows for that platform (LinkedIn → business hours, Twitter/Farcaster → mornings and evenings, DeBank → evenings).
If there's no data yet: you'll see a generic fallback — "Generally, weekday mornings (9-11am) and evenings (7-9pm) have the highest response rates on social media."
